Much of this work on transformations has, however, been restricted to the perceptual level, that is, pupils have been given opportunities to physically manipulate figures using cut-outs, paper folding, geoboards, tracings, etc. Learners are seldom challenged to transfer their perceptual understanding to a representational level, that is, to perform the operations mentally. It is this ability to perform transformations mentally that is valuable in mathematics.

Similarity is studied informally in Grade 9 and more formally in Grade 12, but the transformation dimension (enlargement) is not explored.

Transformations are not, however, restricted to isometric transformations and enlargements.
